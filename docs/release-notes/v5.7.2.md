# DupeZ v5.7.2 — WiFi Disruption Regression Fix

If you updated to v5.7 and found that DISRUPT stopped working on WiFi devices (Xbox, PS5, other PCs) — where it used to disconnect and lag them — this release fixes it.

## What broke

v5.6.5 changed how DupeZ handles a target on your WiFi network. It switched to "self-disrupt" mode by default, which only affects YOUR PC's traffic to/from the target — never the target device itself. So clicking DISRUPT on an Xbox did nothing to the Xbox.

That was the wrong call. When you scan your network, see the devices, and click DISRUPT on one, you want to disrupt *that device*. v5.7.2 restores that.

## What's fixed

- **Same-WiFi targets are disrupted directly again.** DupeZ ARP-spoofs the target so its traffic routes through your PC, then the disruption modules (drop, lag, throttle, disconnect) act on it. This is the pre-v5.7 behavior that worked.
- **The isolation watchdog still has your back.** If your router has AP client isolation enabled (common on Eero, Google Nest, some ISP gateways, guest WiFi), the spoof can't land. DupeZ detects this within 8 seconds and automatically falls back to self-disrupt mode, with a toast telling you what happened. No more silent no-ops — either the cut works, or you're told why it can't.
- **Watchdog tuned** — grace window raised from 5s to 8s so a target console briefly idle in a menu isn't mistaken for AP isolation.

## If a cut still has no effect after updating

1. Check Npcap is installed — Tools → Diagnostics will tell you.
2. If you see an "AP isolation detected" toast, your router is dropping the spoof. Either disable "Client Isolation" / "AP Isolation" in the router's WiFi settings, or connect the DupeZ PC by Ethernet — a wired uplink isn't subject to WiFi client isolation.
3. To deliberately lag only your own connection to a target, set `_force_self_disrupt` in the preset params.
